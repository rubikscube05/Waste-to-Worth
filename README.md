## 📌 Project Overview
Effective resource management is essential for modern, sustainable institutions. This project addresses the critical issue of unused and discarded campus assets—such as furniture, utensils, and equipment—that accumulate over time due to poor inventory systems and administrative bottlenecks. The goal of this initiative is to develop a structured, sustainable approach to asset management that promotes resource optimization, reduces waste, and restores campus aesthetics.

## ⚠️ The Problem
* **Asset Accumulation:** Functional wooden and metal items are often left in open spaces to decay, corrode, or rot over years of neglect.
* **Administrative Bottlenecks:** Hall offices lack the direct authority to clear or dispose of unused assets. The disposal process requires formal, slow-moving approval from the central institute.
* **Environmental & Financial Impact:** Without timely intervention, reusable materials end up as landfill instead of being repurposed or recycled, resulting in financial losses and an increased environmental footprint.

## 🔍 Execution & Methodology
* **Site Surveys:** Conducted extensive audits across five residential halls (Halls 3, 5, 6, 12, and 13) to document and quantify the accumulated waste.
* **Data Categorization:** Classified the discarded items (primarily chairs, tables/desks, bed frames, and doors/windows) by condition. 
* **Staff Interviews:** Engaged with hall office staff to identify the procedural constraints preventing efficient asset disposal.
* **Documentation:** Compiled a consolidated report featuring photographic evidence and data visualizations to present to the institute administration.

## 📊 Key Findings
Our survey revealed that a significant portion of the discarded inventory could be salvaged:
* **45%** of the items required only minor repairs.
* **20%** of the items were completely reusable.
* **35%** of the items were damaged beyond use.

## 💡 Proposed Solutions & Impact
To resolve this issue systematically, we proposed the following actionable framework to the administration:

1. **Sustainable Asset Management Committee:** Form a dedicated committee consisting of hall office representatives and students to oversee asset reuse, repair, and disposal across all halls.
2. **Delegated Authority:** Grant limited decision-making authority directly to hall offices for the timely repair and disposal of accumulated materials.
3. **Repurposing & Recycling:** 
   * Utilize slightly damaged wood for fillers or smaller projects.
   * Restore or repurpose corroded metal components.
   * Sell items completely beyond repair to scrap dealers, utilizing the earnings to fund cleaning efforts and free up campus space.
